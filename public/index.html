How the Raffle Works

Each participant is randomly assigned teams from the 48 nations, then roots for their countries through the entire tournament. Three place tiers — bragging rights on the line.

🎲

Random Team Assignment

Every participant is randomly assigned teams/countries from the field of 48. The number of teams each player receives depends on how many people are in the raffle — the more players, the fewer teams per person.

🥇

1st Place

If one of your assigned countries wins the FIFA World Cup Final and lifts the trophy, you win 1st place. This is the top prize.

🥈

2nd Place

The player holding the World Cup runner-up team wins 2nd place. If that nation belongs to the same player who already won 1st place, 2nd place goes to the player holding the highest FIFA-ranked team remaining.

🥉

3rd Place

The team that wins the 3rd-place match (the game between the two semi-final losers) earns 3rd place. If the winner of 3rd place also has a team that wins 1st or second place, only the higher placement counts. Third place then goes to the highest ranking team not assigned by 1st place or 2nd place winner.

📋

One Place Per Player

If you're lucky enough to hold teams that finish in multiple positions, only your highest placement counts. The next tier rolls down to the next eligible player, so every place goes to a different person.

⚖️ Key Rules

  • Each player is randomly assigned an equal share of the 48 teams via the raffle draw.
  • If a single player holds both the 1st and 2nd place finishers, only 1st place is awarded to them. 2nd place then goes to the player holding the highest FIFA-ranked team among all other players.
  • 3rd place goes to the team that wins the 3rd place match. If the winner of 3rd place also has a team that wins 1st or second place, only the higher placement counts. Third place then goes to the highest ranking team not assigned by 1st place or 2nd place winner.
  • If you hold multiple finishers in the top three, only your best placement counts. Lower placements roll to the next eligible player.
