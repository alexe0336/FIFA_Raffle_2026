How the Raffle Works

Each participant is randomly assigned 8 teams from the 48 nations competing. Root for your countries through the entire tournament — three places awarded.

🎲

Team Assignment

Every participant draws 8 teams from the field of 48 via the seeded draw shown above. Each of the 12 groups is drained evenly so no single group is gutted before another is touched.

🥇

1st Place

The entrant holding the team that wins the World Cup Final wins 1st place.

🥈

2nd Place

The entrant holding the runner-up wins 2nd place — unless that entrant also holds 1st place. In that case the tiebreaker rules below apply.

🥉

3rd Place

The entrant holding the winner of the 3rd-place match wins 3rd place. If they already hold a higher placement, the same roll-down logic applies.

⚖️ Tiebreaker for Second Place

If the entrant holding First Place would, under the standard scoring rules, also hold the team that lost the Final Match, then Second Place shall instead be awarded as follows:

  • Compare the teams that lost Match 61 and Match 62. Second Place is awarded to the entrant holding the losing team with the greater number of goals scored in its respective match.
  • If those two losing teams scored an equal number of goals, the following criteria apply in this exact order, each resolving the tie before the next is consulted:
    • (1) Higher possession percentage
    • (2) More shots on goal
    • (3) More total shot attempts
    • (4) Fewer fouls
    • (5) Fewer yellow cards
    • (6) Fewer red cards
    • (7) More corner kicks
    • (8) More saves
  • If both losing teams of Matches 61 and 62 are held by the First-Place entrant, apply subsections (a)–(b) to the teams that lost Matches 57, 58, 59, and 60, ranked first by goals scored and then by the criteria in (b).
  • The same procedure applies where the First-Place or Second-Place entrant also holds the winner of the Third-Place Match: that placement is reassigned using subsections (a)–(b).
  • Final backstop. If two or more teams remain tied after every criterion above, the tie shall be resolved however the organizer of the raffle determines, which is final and binding.
